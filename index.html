FAQ

Common Questions About
Business Process Automation

What is business process automation?

Business process automation (BPA) means replacing repetitive manual tasks - data entry, lead routing, follow-ups, document filing - with software systems that do the same work faster, without errors, and without needing a human to trigger them.

How is this different from just using Zapier or Make?

Tools like Zapier and n8n are part of the stack, but the value is in the logic design - knowing which conditions to check, how to handle failures, how to route data correctly, and how to integrate AI into the workflow. The tools are just how you build it.

How long does it take to build an automation system?

Most systems I build go live within one to two weeks. Complex multi-workflow systems with AI integration and custom CRM connections can take three to four weeks. I don't rush - I build for reliability first.

Do I need technical knowledge to use the systems you build?

No. Every system ships with full documentation, and the interfaces are designed so your team can operate them without automation experience. If anything breaks or needs updating, I'm available , but most systems run without intervention.

What kinds of businesses do you work with?

Primarily small businesses - law firms, moving companies, therapy practices, landscaping companies, consultants, field service operators. If your business has a manual process that runs daily, it's almost certainly automatable.

What happens after the system is built?

You receive full documentation and ownership of everything - workflows, code, credentials. I offer ongoing maintenance and monitoring, but you're not locked in. The system is yours to run, modify, or hand to another developer.
